Generative Melodies.


What makes music sound "good" is simply a set of rules built up inside our brains based off of the music we have heard before in our lives. Rules such as "Chords are usually two stacked thirds" or "The V chord goes to the I chord" or "ti goes to do". These are rules that you learn in music theory classes that allow you to realize why in a particular instance your expectations are met, and rules are followed, or your expectations, and the rules, are broken. This program's aim is to allow you to edit the rules and see what the effect on music might be. Which rules should be the most important? which ones should be forgotten about? Should "harmonic content" be defined differently? Leaps and jumps used in different frequencies? You decide.
