Divining Alabama

2019
violin + electronics



The number of reported pregnancy-related deaths in the United States steadily increased from 7.2 deaths per 100,000 live births in 1987 to 16.9 deaths per 100,000 live births in 2016.
...
Each year in the United States, about 700 women die of pregnancy-related causes, and more than 50,000 have severe pregnancy complications.

-- from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention

In 1966, the leader of Romania, Nicolae Ceausescu, outlawed access to abortion and contraception in a bid to boost the country’s population.
...
As a last resort, many Romanian women turned to home and back-alley abortions, and by 1989, an estimated 10,000 women had died as a result of unsafe procedures.

-- from Amy Mackinnon, staff writer at Foreign Policy

With these facts in mind, let us attempt to divine Alabama.

The violin line in this piece tracks the maternal mortality rate in Romania from 1950 to 2015 and highlights the communist party’s policy of a total ban on abortion from 1967 to 1989.

Max/MSP patch available as raw files or a standalone application. Also performable as a fixed media piece with this backing track.
