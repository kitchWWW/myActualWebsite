Brian Ellis.

Brian Ellis is an Austin based composer, researcher, and guitarist. He is currently enrolled at the University of Texas at Austin where he is a Computer Science Honors and Music double major. His musical interests lie in using code to realize his larger compositional vision:

The performance of a work is always unique, and the score should work to facilitate this.

Brian has had works premiered in numerous venues, including the Ears Eyes and Feet concert series, with the Good House Collective, in the Engineering Chamber Orchestra, as well as in numerous student concerts at the University of Texas.

His musical influences include artists from many areas including David Lang and friends, The Show Ponies, Mauro Giuliani, David Cope's Emmy, and John Williams - primarily the guitarist but on occasion the film score composer as well.

As a Computer Scientist, Brian has interest in artificial intelligence and algorithms for genomics, big data, and machine learning. He works in Howard Ochman's lab on this project, and as a code monkey makes chrome apps and tells computers how to spit out music. Work can be viewed on gitHub and more information found on his work website or resume.
